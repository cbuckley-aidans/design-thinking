PROMPT ENGINEERING

TECHNIQUES

Crafting Effective Design Iteration Prompts

1

Prompt Structure

A well-structured prompt for design iteration includes these key components:

Original Context

Clearly describe what your original design is showing and its purpose

Aesthetic Target

Specify the exact aesthetic style using precise terminology

Element Focus

Indicate which elements should be transformed or preserved

Technical Requirements

Specify any constraints like perspective, proportion, or medium

Basic Prompt Template:

Generate an image that transforms my [type of design] to have a [specific aesthetic style] look while maintaining [key elements to preserve]. Focus on altering [specific elements] to reflect [aesthetic characteristics]. Ensure the perspective remains [perspective type].
2

Aesthetic Terminology

Using precise aesthetic terminology is crucial for getting targeted results:

Romanesque

Round arches, thick walls, small windows

Brutalist

Raw concrete, geometric forms, monolithic

Art Deco

Geometric patterns, bold colors, luxurious

Neoclassical

Columns, symmetry, grandeur, proportion

Deconstructivist

Fragmentation, non-rectilinear shapes

Cubist

Multiple viewpoints, geometric fragmentation

Bauhaus

Form follows function, minimalist, geometric

Memphis Design

Bold patterns, bright colors, geometric

Minimalist

Simplified forms, limited color palette

Futurist

Dynamic movement, technology, speed emphasis

Scandinavian

Simple, functional, natural materials, light

Biomimetic

Nature-inspired, organic curves, natural patterns

SCAMPER IDEATION

TECHNIQUE

Using SCAMPER with AI Image Generation

1

SCAMPER Framework

SCAMPER is an ideation technique that can be combined with AI image generation to produce creative design variations:

S - Substitute

Replace elements with alternatives

"Generate an image of my design with [material] substituted with [alternative]"

C - Combine

Merge elements or ideas

"Create a version of my design that combines [style 1] and [style 2]"

A - Adapt

Adjust for a different context

"Adapt my design to a [different context] setting with appropriate elements"

M - Modify

Change elements, scale, or color

"Modify my design to be more [descriptive term] with dramatic proportions"

P - Purpose

Use for a different function

"Transform my design into a [different function] with appropriate features"

E - Eliminate

Remove elements or simplify

"Create a version of my design with all [specific elements] removed"

R - Reverse/Rearrange

Change order, layout, or orientation

"Generate an image of my design with the [specific arrangement] reversed or rearranged"

COMPLETE PROMPT TEMPLATE

TEMPLATE

Comprehensive SCAMPER Prompt for Design Iteration

1

Complete SCAMPER Prompt Template

Use this comprehensive prompt template to apply SCAMPER thinking to your design iteration:

Complete SCAMPER Design Prompt

I've uploaded an image of my [type of design]. Please generate variations that explore the following design transformations:

Substitute: Replace [element] with [alternative material/element]
Combine: Merge the aesthetics of [style 1] with [style 2]
Adapt: Transform the design to suit [different context/environment]
Modify: Change the [proportion/scale/color] to be more [descriptive term]
Purpose: Reimagine it as [different product/building type]
Eliminate: Remove [specific elements] for a cleaner/simpler look
Reverse/Rearrange: Invert the [specific arrangement] of the design

Important requirements:

  • Maintain the original [important elements to preserve]
  • Keep the [perspective/viewpoint] consistent
  • Ensure the [key function/feature] remains usable

Tip: Replace the bracketed text with specifics relevant to your design. You can use all or only selected elements of the SCAMPER framework depending on your project needs.

2

Example Application

Here's how you might apply this to a product design:

Example: Chair Design Iteration

I've uploaded an image of my minimalist chair design. Please generate variations that explore the following design transformations:

Substitute: Replace the wooden frame with bent metal tubing
Combine: Merge the aesthetics of Scandinavian design with Memphis Design elements
Adapt: Transform the design to suit an outdoor garden setting
Modify: Change the proportions to be more dramatic and sculptural
Purpose: Reimagine it as a rocking chair with appropriate modifications
Eliminate: Remove all unnecessary structural elements for a more minimalist approach
Reverse/Rearrange: Invert the relationship between the seat and backrest

Important requirements:

  • Maintain the ergonomic comfort and appropriate seat height
  • Keep the side profile view consistent
  • Ensure the chair remains functional and stable
