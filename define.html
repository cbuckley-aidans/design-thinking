Creating Design Criteria

Design criteria help you measure how successful your design is. They are like a checklist for your design. Aim to create around 4-6 design criteria for your design. You will evaluate these at the end of your assessment item, so ensure they are well-thought out.

Oftentimes, the client will describe what they need. You can easily reword their requirements into clear design criteria.

Design Criteria Types

1

Specific Criteria

Be clear and precise. Use clear numbers and measurements instead of vague terms.

  • ❌ Avoid: "The design must be light"
    ✓ Better: "The design must weigh less than 500g"
2

Measurable Criteria

Your criteria must be testable. You need to be able to verify if your design meets the criteria.

  • ❌ Avoid: "Must be strong"
    ✓ Better: "Must survive a 1m drop test"
3

Achievable Criteria

Make sure you can actually meet the criteria with your available resources and skills.

  • ❌ Avoid: "Made from gold"
    ✓ Better: "Made from recycled plastic"
4

Relevant Criteria

Each criterion should matter to your user and solve their problem.

  • ❌ Avoid: "Beach toy must be fireproof"
    ✓ Better: "Beach toy must be water resistant"

Dieter Rams' 10 Principles

Dieter Rams was a famous designer who developed 10 Principles of Good Design to follow. These principles can help guide and narrow down your design criteria further. Use these in combination with what the client wants.

1

Good design is innovative

Creates new solutions without copying others

Must include at least one feature not found in existing products

2

Makes a product useful

Focuses on function over looks

Must complete its main task in under 30 seconds

3

Is aesthetic

Looks good while working well

8/10 users must rate appearance as "appealing" or higher

4

Makes a product understandable

Easy to figure out how to use

New users must be able to use without instructions

5

Is unobtrusive

Simple and not flashy

Must use no more than 3 colours in design

6

Is honest

Doesn't pretend to be more than it is

All materials must be clearly labeled

7

Is long-lasting

Built to last, not disposable

Must last 5 years of normal use

8

Is thorough

Every detail matters

All edges must be rounded to 2mm radius

9

Is environmentally friendly

Considers environmental impact

Must be made from 90% recyclable materials

10

Is as little design as possible

No unnecessary features

Must have no features that aren't used weekly

Example Criteria

Below are examples of clear, specific, and measurable design criteria. These examples show how to write criteria that can be properly evaluated at the end of your project.

1

Innovative

Description
My water bottle design will be innovative. It will fit in a standard car cup holder while also being combinable to create a larger thermos.
Measure
I will know I have reached this outcome by being able to consistently combine and detach my bottle.
2

Useful

Description
My laptop stand will prioritize function. It will be adjustable to three heights for ergonomic typing and fit laptops up to 16 inches.
Measure
The design will meet the goal if it adjusts to all three heights and holds a 16-inch laptop securely without wobbling.
3

Aesthetic

Description
My desk organizer will have a sleek, modern look. It will feature a minimalist black-and-white design with hidden storage compartments.
Measure
The goal is achieved if my client gives me positive feedback in a survey.
4

Environmentally Friendly

Description
My shopping bag design will focus on sustainability. It will use 90% recycled materials and be biodegradable within five years.
Measure
The design will be successful if all materials used are certified as 90% recycled.
