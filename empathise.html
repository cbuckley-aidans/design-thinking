WHAT IS EMPATHISING?

Empathising means understanding others' needs and feelings by putting yourself in their shoes. It's about creating a connection with users to design better solutions for them.

EMPATHY ELEMENTS

1

ACTIVE LISTENING

Pay attention to what people say, how they say it, and what they don't mention.

  • Listen without interrupting
  • Note tone and emotions
  • Observe body language
  • Ask clarifying questions
2

UNDERSTANDING CONTEXT

Each user has different circumstances that affect how they use products.

  • Environmental factors
  • Time constraints
  • Social situations
  • Technical limitations
3

SETTING ASIDE ASSUMPTIONS

Put aside your own ideas to truly see things from the user's perspective.

  • Acknowledge biases
  • Question certainties
  • Remain curious
  • Seek diverse perspectives
4

RECOGNIZING EMOTIONS

Understanding how users feel when using your product is as important as knowing what they do with it.

  • Frustrations and pain points
  • Moments of delight
  • Anxiety triggers
  • Confidence builders

UNDERSTANDING THROUGH OBSERVATION

Observation is crucial for understanding how people actually use products in real-world situations. People often can't articulate exactly how they use products, but observation reveals unconscious behaviors and workarounds.

1

WHY OBSERVE?

  • Reveals unconscious habits
  • Uncovers environmental factors
  • Shows actual usage vs reported usage
  • Identifies unexpected pain points
2

DIRECT EXPERIENCE

Try using the product yourself in different situations to spot problems firsthand.

  • Use in various environments
  • Try with different constraints
  • Note your own frustrations
3

SHADOW USERS

Watch people use the product without interfering. Note where they struggle or find workarounds.

  • Observe without interrupting
  • Note hesitations and confusion
  • Track time spent on tasks
4

DOCUMENT EVERYTHING

Take photos, videos, and notes to capture how people use products in real situations.

  • Capture the environment
  • Record user reactions
  • Note unexpected uses

CLIENT ROLEPLAYING

Watch clients use their current solution to spot problems they might not mention. Roleplaying helps you understand the full context of how people interact with products and services.

1

START WITH REAL TASKS

Ask clients to show you how they complete actual work tasks with their current tools - not hypothetical scenarios.

2

ENCOURAGE THINKING ALOUD

Ask the client to verbalize their thoughts as they work through tasks to understand their mental model.

3

NOTE HESITATIONS

Pay attention to moments when the client pauses or appears confused - these indicate potential pain points.

4

IDENTIFY WORKAROUNDS

Look for creative solutions the client has developed to handle limitations in their current tools.

5

LISTEN TO TERMINOLOGY

Note the specific words clients use to describe features and functions - use these in your design.

EFFECTIVE INTERVIEWING

Good questions reveal more about what users really need. Interviews help you understand the motivations, goals, and frustrations that observation alone might miss.

1

ASK OPEN QUESTIONS

Instead of "Do you like this?" ask "How do you feel about this?"

  • Starts with how, what, why
  • Encourages detailed answers
  • Avoids yes/no responses
2

FIND PAIN POINTS

Ask "What frustrates you most when using this?"

  • Identifies improvement areas
  • Reveals priorities
  • Uncovers workarounds
3

LEARN ABOUT CONTEXT

Ask "When and where do you use this most?"

  • Reveals usage environment
  • Identifies constraints
  • Shows integration points
4

DISCOVER WISHES

Ask "What would make this perfect for you?"

  • Reveals desired features
  • Uncovers aspirations
  • Identifies value opportunities

EXAMPLE INTERVIEW QUESTIONS

1

ABOUT USE

  • "How do you typically use this product?"
  • "Where and when do you use it most?"
  • "Who else is involved in using it?"
2

PROBLEMS

  • "What's the most annoying part?"
  • "What would you change first?"
  • "Tell me about a time it didn't work well."
3

GOALS

  • "What are you trying to achieve?"
  • "How do you know if you've succeeded?"
  • "Why is this important to you?"
4

ALTERNATIVES

  • "What other solutions have you tried?"
  • "How do you work around limitations?"
  • "What would make you switch products?"
