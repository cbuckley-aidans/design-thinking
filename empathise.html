What is Empathising?

Empathising means putting yourself in someone else's shoes to truly understand their needs, feelings, and experiences.

Active Listening

Great designers are excellent listeners. They pay attention not just to what people say, but how they say it and what remains unsaid.

Understanding Context

Every user has unique circumstances, challenges, and environments that affect how they interact with products.

Setting Aside Assumptions

Empathising requires putting aside our own biases and preconceptions to truly understand others' perspectives.

Understanding Through Observation

Observation is crucial for understanding how people actually use products in real-world situations.

Direct Experience

Use the product yourself in different situations and environments to understand its limitations firsthand.

Shadow Users

Watch how different people interact with the product without interfering. Note their struggles and workarounds.

Document Everything

Take photos, videos, and notes of how people use the product in different contexts and situations.

Effective Interviewing

Good interviews reveal deeper insights about user needs and desires.

Ask Open Questions

Instead of "Do you like this feature?" ask "How do you feel about this feature?"

Explore Pain Points

Ask "What frustrates you most when using this product?"

Investigate Context

Ask "When and where do you typically use this product?"

Future Desires

Ask "What would make this product perfect for your needs?"
