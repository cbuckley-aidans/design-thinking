What is Empathy in Design?

Empathy is the ability to understand and share the feelings of another person. In design, this means:

  • Observing: How do people actually use products?
  • Listening: What are their frustrations and needs?
  • Feeling: What emotions do they experience?
  • Understanding: What's their full context and story?

Key insight: Even "good" designs can exclude people if we make assumptions about who will use them!

Empathy Considers

  • Physical abilities
  • Emotional needs
  • Cultural context
  • Life circumstances

The Empathy Challenge

Why Is Empathy Hard?

Designers often make products for people like themselves, but what happens when users are different?

We Assume
  • Everyone is like us
  • Our way is the "normal" way
  • Good design works for everyone
Reality Is
  • People are incredibly diverse
  • There's no single "normal" user
  • Assumptions can exclude people

See-Think-Wonder Framework

Use this thinking routine to deeply explore the controller design

SEE

What do you notice?

  • Shape and curves
  • Grips and texture
  • Trigger design
  • Overall size and weight

THINK

What design choices were made?

WONDER

What questions do you have?

  • Could everyone use this easily?
  • What if you had limited mobility?
  • What if you were left-handed?
  • What about different hand sizes?

Critical Thinking Challenge

The Big Question

This controller is considered well-designed...
But could it work for EVERYONE?

Consider:

  • What if someone couldn't hold it the "standard" way?
  • What if they had different hand mobility or strength?
  • What if the button placement didn't work for their needs?
  • How might they feel being excluded from gaming with friends?

1

The Empathy Realisation

Even though the standard PS5 controller was carefully designed, Sony realised something important:

The Problem
  • Traditional controllers assume all users have the same physical abilities
  • Button placement and grip design excluded many gamers
  • Gaming became frustrating or impossible for some people
  • Social gaming excluded friends with disabilities
The Response

They decided to work collaboratively with the disability community to understand the real challenges and design a different solution.

Empathy Design Thinking Canvas

Work through these steps for your product

Step 1: Identify Users
  • Who might use this product?
  • What ages, abilities, and lifestyles?
  • What different contexts might they use it in?
Step 2: Understand Needs
  • What are their main goals?
  • What frustrates them about current versions?
  • What would make their experience better?
Step 3: Generate Ideas
  • What features would help different users?
  • How could you make it more inclusive?
  • What design choices show empathy?
Step 4: Design Decisions
  • What specific changes would you make?
  • How do these help different user groups?
  • What trade-offs might you need to consider?
3

Empathy Reflection Questions

Think about your design process

  • Which user group was hardest to design for? Why?
  • What assumptions did you initially make about who would use this product?
  • How did considering different user groups change your ideas?
  • What design features could benefit multiple user groups at once?
  • How might you test your ideas with real users?

Key Takeaways

What We've Discovered Today

  • Empathy is the foundation of inclusive design - understanding real user experiences drives innovation
  • Even "good" designs can exclude people when we make assumptions about users
  • True empathy requires considering diverse needs: age, ability, lifestyle, culture, and aesthetics
  • Accessible design often benefits everyone, not just the target users
  • Design thinking starts with understanding people's emotions and experiences
