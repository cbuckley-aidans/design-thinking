What is Low-Fidelity Prototyping?

Low-fidelity prototyping means creating quick, rough versions of your ideas using simple materials and techniques.

Why Prototype First?

The Problem

Imagine spending weeks building something, only to discover:

  • Users find it confusing
  • Important features are missing
  • It doesn't solve the real problem
  • You have to start over!

The Solution

With prototyping, you discover these issues early:

  • Test ideas quickly
  • Get feedback fast
  • Make changes easily
  • Build confidence in your design

Remember: It's better to fail fast and learn, than to fail slow and expensive!

Prototyping Guidelines

Time Limit

You have 35 minutes to create your prototype. Take your time to think through your design!

Focus on Criteria

Make sure your prototype addresses each design criterion listed.

Rough is Good

This isn't about artistic skill - focus on function and ideas!

Remember: The goal is to test your ideas, not create a masterpiece!

3

Prototype Creation Checklist

While you're creating, ask yourself:

Does my prototype clearly show the main function?
Have I addressed each design criterion?
Can someone else understand what this is supposed to do?
Would this actually solve the problem described?
