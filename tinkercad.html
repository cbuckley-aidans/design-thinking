Basic Controls

When you open up your first 3D design, you'll need to quickly figure out how to get around. Practice these controls first!

Pan (Moving Around)

Shift + +

Hold Shift + Right Click + Drag

Rotate

+

Right Click + Drag

Zoom

Scroll Wheel (Zoom Gesture on Laptop Trackpad) or + / - Keys

Getting Started

Learn how to add shapes and modify their properties - the foundation of 3D design!

Step 1: Select Your Shape

Choose a basic shape from the shapes panel on the right side of your screen.

❮ ❯

Step 2: Position Your Shape

Click and drag to position the shape. Use the top arrow to adjust its height.

❮ ❯

Step 3: Modify Properties

Adjust the shape's size using the white boxes or change values in the sidebar.

❮ ❯

Making Holes

Learn how to create holes in shapes to create more complex shapes for your prototyping.

Step 1: Create Two Shapes

Drag two shapes onto your workplane - for example, a cube and a cylinder.

❮ ❯

Step 2: Position the Hole

Position the cylinder where you want the hole to be. Make sure it intersects with the cube.

❮ ❯

Step 3: Create the Hole

Select the cylinder and click the "Hole" button or press 'H' on your keyboard. The cylinder will turn see-through, indicating it's now a hole.

❮ ❯

Step 4: Group to make the hole

Select ALL of the objects now. Click the group button or hit CTRL + G on your keyboard to group them together. This will create a hole.

❮ ❯

Grouping Objects

Learn how to combine shapes and align them perfectly using groups and grid snapping!

Step 1: Create Multiple Shapes

Add several shapes to your workspace. Hold Shift while moving them to snap to the grid for perfect alignment.

❮ ❯

Step 2: Align Your Objects

Use the alignment tools in the top toolbar (or press L) to perfectly align objects. Select multiple objects by holding Shift while clicking.

❮ ❯

Step 3: Group Objects

Select all objects you want to group, then press Ctrl+G (or click the Group button). They'll now move and rotate as one unit. You can click the ungroup button to reset this.

❮ ❯

Keyboard Shortcuts

The best designers use keyboard shortcuts to save time from having to click icons!

Viewing 3D Space

  • F: Fit selection into view
  • Ctrl + Left mouse: Orbit
  • Shift + Right mouse: Pan
  • +/- or Scroll: Zoom

Shape Properties

  • Ctrl + G: Group
  • Ctrl + Shift + G: Ungroup
  • H: Make a Hole
  • S: Make Solid Color
  • T: Make Transparent
  • Ctrl + L: Lock/Unlock

Commands

  • Ctrl + C: Copy
  • Ctrl + V: Paste
  • Ctrl + D: Duplicate
  • Alt + Move: Drag copy
  • Ctrl + Z: Undo
  • Ctrl + Y: Redo

Helpers

  • R: Place Ruler
  • W: Place Workplane
  • Shift + W: Place workplane at shape
  • E: Show Shape Workplane

Movement & Scaling

  • Shift + Rotate: Snap to 45°
  • Alt + Scale: Scale about center
  • Shift + Scale: Uniform scale
  • Arrows: Move 1 grid space
  • Ctrl + Arrows: Move up/down
